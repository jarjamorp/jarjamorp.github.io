Covid Quarantine

Traveling home to Australia during Covid was pretty inconvenient.

There was a cap at 4,000 people per week that could enter Australia. Authorities claimed 35,000 people wanted to get home. Airlines, based on their numbers, said it was more like 100,000. Seats on repatriation flights would go immeidately upon becoming available, like a music festival. After a few attempts refreshing the screen at the time We landed in the desert in Darwin, Northern Territory, met by a sea of hazmat suits and swabs. After a stern, but muffled, lecture of the seriousness of the situation, we were loaded onto a bus and driven to a quarantine camp. A large camp of small prefab units, rows and rows of them plonked on the hot red dirt under a hot blue sky.
