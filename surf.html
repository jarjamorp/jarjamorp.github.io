Surfing

I apparently first stood up on a surfboard when I was 3. My old man was a part-time surf coach when I grew up, so I kind of had to surf. Fine by me.

I would run bare foot to the beach almost every morning and surf with my mates. On weekends I would help my old man teach tourists how to surf, or rather fall off. They loved it.

Surfing has taken me to many strange and beautiful places that I otherwise would never have gone. Tiny villages in Chile, with horse and carts on the road. Living on a beach for 3 months in Mexico for $4 a night, with no responsibilities besides staying alive in the surf and on the street. Island hopping in Indo on mopeds, dodging cows and trucks and cops, racing to catch the swell.

Surfing is freedom, exercise, meditation, an infinite game. There aint no other drug like overcoming fear, followed by an immediate reward like making a critical drop or getting spat out. I am happy when I am in the ocean, not a soul in sight, no technology, just me and the sea, thoughts and nature.
