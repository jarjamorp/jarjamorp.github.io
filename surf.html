Surfing

I first stood up on a surfboard when I was about as tall as one of my legs is long today. Surfing was my thing growing up. On weekends, I would help my old man teach kooks how to surf. Most mornings, I would run bare foot to the beach to surf with my mates, the ocean a sunlit deep bluegreen. When I could drive, I would go before dawn to the heavier waves, get smashed, get back to school just in time, and proceed to drench my books with my leaky tap nose.

Surfing has led me to many strange and beautiful places that I otherwise would never have gone. Tiny villages in Chile with horse-drawn carriages on the road. Living on a beach for months in Mexico at $4 a night, with no responsibilities besides staying alive in the surf and on the street. Island hopping in Indo on mopeds, dodging cows and trucks and cops, racing to catch the swell.

Surfing is freedom. Exercise. Meditation. An infinite game. Aint no drug like just making the drop and getting spat out right in front of your mate. There are no words for the joy of being in the ocean, nobody around, no technology, no buildings, just me and the sea, thoughts and nature.
