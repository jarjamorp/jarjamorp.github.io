Dead of Night

On the road between the two "cities" of Tasmania, there is a farm filled with dead trees. I tended to notice them as I drove by.

One random night I somehow decide to drive down to this dead tree farm with my camera and a construction light. I sneak my car off the road and hop the farmer's fence, feeling impish, as a vivid memory rings in my mind of the time I got chased off a Tasmanian farm by the gunshot of the farmer (not without reason).

As I walk cautiously toward to the first dead tree, I suddenly feel the hairs on the back on my neck go crazy. I freeze like the grass under my boot. I'm being stared at, by a dozen pairs of eyes, looking direclty at me, floating in complete darkness, blinking.

Sheep.

I fumble around setting up the construction light, underneath a dead tree in the pitch black. "Ok", I say to no one in particular, "that looks ok." I turn on the construction light while simultaneously keeping an eye on the farmer's house, hoping his light doesn't also turn on. Nothing. Good.

I line up the shot and press the magic button.

No click. Tried again. Nothing. As my freezing fingers fiddle with all my camera's knobs and settings, I sigh profanity. But neither fiddling nor profanity avails.

So at this rather strange moment, shivering on a farm at 1 am, in the company of creepy sheep and creepier trees, my camera is bricked.

Lucky I had my phone.
