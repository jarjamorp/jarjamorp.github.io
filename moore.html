Moore Upon Moore

The exponential function $f(x) = e^{x}$, where $e$ approximates 2.71828, represents a mathematical construct in which the rate of change is proportional to the function's current value. This function is a specific case of the general exponential function, with $e$ as its base, known for its unique mathematical properties. The constant $e$, also referred to as Euler's number, is the base rate of growth shared by all continually growing processes. In this context, the function $e^{x}$ exemplifies natural exponential growth, where the variable $x$ denotes the exponent.

Differentiation of $f(x) = e^{x}$ yields $\frac{d}{dx}e^{x}=e^{x}$, illustrating that the derivative of $e^{x}$ w.r.t $x$ is $e^{x}$ itself. This self-referential property signifies that the rate of increase of the function is directly proportional to its present value. The function's graph steeply ascends as $x$ increases, growing exponentially.
